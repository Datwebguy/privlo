# Privlo — 3-minute pitch

## 0:00–0:30 — Problem

“Payroll, investor allocations, airdrops, and vesting have moved onchain—but
their privacy has not. Public amounts expose salaries, treasury strategy, and
investor positions. Teams are forced to choose between composability and
confidentiality.”

Show the landing page and the “Public rails. Private financial data.” section.

## 0:30–0:55 — Solution

“Privlo is a privacy-first control center for financial flows. Issuers can run
disperse, airdrop, and vesting campaigns while every individual amount remains
encrypted. Recipients see only what belongs to them.”

Open the dashboard and point out encrypted badges and campaign types.

## 0:55–1:40 — Creator demo

1. Open **Create campaign**.
2. Choose **Disperse** for contributor payroll.
3. Explain CSV/manual recipient import.
4. Show the review step and privacy checklist.
5. Execute using TokenOps’ pre-deployed Sepolia singleton.

“Zama encrypts values before submission. TokenOps handles the audited
distribution contracts and batch mechanics. Privlo adds the polished workflow
teams and recipients actually need.”

## 1:40–2:30 — Recipient demo

1. Open **My private claims** with the recipient wallet.
2. Show the concealed amount and “Visible only to you” indicator.
3. Click **Decrypt amount** and approve the scoped Zama permit.
4. Reveal the private amount.
5. Click **Claim** and show the success state plus Sepolia transaction.

“The reveal is explicit and recipient-controlled. The claim uses the original
issuer-signed encrypted payload; plaintext never appears in calldata or
onchain storage.”

## 2:30–2:55 — Why Zama + TokenOps

“Privlo showcases composable privacy for a real financial workflow. Zama FHE
keeps data encrypted during computation. TokenOps gives us confidential
disperse, airdrop, and vesting primitives through production-grade,
pre-deployed contracts. Together, they turn privacy from a cryptography demo
into usable financial infrastructure.”

## 2:55–3:00 — Close

“Privlo: Private Financial Flows. Public rails, private business.”
